# Best Metroidvania Games

A curated guide to the finest Metroidvania games across all platforms.

## What is a Metroidvania?

Metroidvania is a subgenre of action-adventure games that emphasizes exploration, non-linear gameplay, and the acquisition of new abilities that unlock previously inaccessible areas. The term combines "Metroid" and "Castlevania," the two franchises that pioneered this genre.

### Key Characteristics
- **Interconnected world design** with multiple paths and shortcuts
- **Ability-gating** - new powers unlock access to previously unreachable areas
- **Backtracking** encouraged to discover secrets and progress
- **Map system** to track exploration progress
- **Character progression** through upgrades and power-ups

## The Classics

### Super Metroid (1994)
**Platform:** SNES

The gold standard that defined the genre. Super Metroid perfected the formula with its atmospheric world, tight controls, and masterful level design. The game respects player intelligence, offering minimal guidance while maintaining a perfect sense of progression.

**Why it's essential:**
- Revolutionary environmental storytelling
- Perfect pacing and difficulty curve
- Iconic soundtrack and atmosphere
- Speedrunning community favorite

### Castlevania: Symphony of the Night (1997)
**Platform:** PlayStation, Saturn

Alucard's adventure through Dracula's castle introduced RPG elements to the Metroidvania formula. With its gothic aesthetic, extensive equipment system, and the famous inverted castle twist, SotN remains a masterpiece.

**Why it's essential:**
- Deep RPG mechanics and character customization
- Gorgeous 2D sprite work
- Memorable soundtrack by Michiru Yamane
- Massive replayability

## Modern Masterpieces

### Hollow Knight (2017)
**Platform:** PC, Switch, PlayStation, Xbox

Team Cherry's debut is a hauntingly beautiful journey through the fallen kingdom of Hallownest. With challenging combat, intricate lore, and over 40 hours of content, Hollow Knight sets the modern standard.

**Highlights:**
- Stunning hand-drawn art style
- Challenging but fair boss battles
- Deep lore hidden in environmental details
- Extensive post-launch content
- Incredible value for the price

### Ori and the Blind Forest (2015) & Ori and the Will of the Wisps (2020)
**Platform:** PC, Xbox, Switch

Moon Studios created two of the most visually stunning and emotionally resonant Metroidvanias. The Ori games feature fluid movement, challenging platforming, and breathtaking art direction.

**Highlights:**
- Gorgeous hand-painted visuals
- Emotional storytelling
- Smooth, responsive controls
- Beautiful orchestral soundtrack
- Innovative escape sequences

### Metroid Dread (2021)
**Platform:** Nintendo Switch

The long-awaited conclusion to the Metroid saga delivers intense action and exploration. MercurySteam and Nintendo crafted a modern Metroid that honors the series' legacy while introducing new mechanics like the terrifying E.M.M.I. encounters.

**Highlights:**
- Fluid, responsive combat
- Tense stealth sequences
- Classic Metroid exploration
- Stunning visual presentation
- Perfect difficulty balance

## Hidden Gems

### Axiom Verge (2015)
**Platform:** Multi-platform

A love letter to classic Metroid with a glitchy, reality-bending twist. Created by a single developer, Axiom Verge offers unique mechanics and a mysterious sci-fi narrative.

### Environmental Station Alpha (2015)
**Platform:** PC

Don't let the retro graphics fool you—this indie gem features some of the most intricate level design in the genre, with secrets that will challenge even veteran players.

### Blasphemous (2019)
**Platform:** Multi-platform

A dark, brutal Metroidvania set in a nightmarish religious world. Combines challenging Souls-like combat with gorgeous pixel art and disturbing imagery.

### Gato Roboto (2019)
**Platform:** PC, Switch

A bite-sized Metroidvania where you play as a cat piloting a mech suit. Short but sweet, with clever design and Game Boy-inspired visuals.

### Ender Lilies: Quietus of the Knights (2021)
**Platform:** Multi-platform

A hauntingly beautiful dark fantasy Metroidvania with a unique companion-based combat system and gorgeous art.

## Honorable Mentions

### Guacamelee! 1 & 2
**Platform:** Multi-platform

Lucha libre-themed Metroidvanias with vibrant Mexican-inspired art, tight combat, and challenging platforming. The games blend humor with satisfying gameplay.

### Dead Cells
**Platform:** Multi-platform

A roguelike-Metroidvania hybrid ("Roguevania") with procedurally generated levels, fast-paced combat, and permanent death. Each run feels fresh and exciting.

### Salt and Sanctuary
**Platform:** Multi-platform

A 2D Dark Souls-inspired Metroidvania with deep RPG mechanics, challenging combat, and a dark fantasy setting.

### Bloodstained: Ritual of the Night
**Platform:** Multi-platform

Koji Igarashi's spiritual successor to Castlevania delivers a massive gothic castle to explore with deep RPG systems and crafting.

### The Messenger
**Platform:** Multi-platform

Starts as a linear action platformer before transforming into a full Metroidvania. Features time-travel mechanics and excellent humor.

## Getting Started

### For Beginners
Start with:
1. **Ori and the Blind Forest** - Beautiful, accessible, and emotionally engaging
2. **Hollow Knight** - The modern standard, though challenging
3. **Metroid Dread** - If you have a Switch, perfect introduction to the genre

### For Veterans
Challenge yourself with:
1. **Environmental Station Alpha** - Incredibly deep and challenging
2. **Blasphemous** - Punishing combat and dark atmosphere
3. **Axiom Verge** - For those who love classic Metroid

### For Speedrunners
1. **Super Metroid** - The ultimate speedrunning Metroidvania
2. **Hollow Knight** - Multiple categories and routing options
3. **Metroid Dread** - Modern speedrunning favorite

## Essential Features to Look For

When choosing your next Metroidvania, consider:

- **Map Quality**: A good map system is crucial for tracking progress
- **Movement Feel**: Responsive controls make exploration enjoyable
- **Ability Variety**: Diverse powers keep gameplay fresh
- **World Design**: Interconnected areas with meaningful shortcuts
- **Boss Quality**: Memorable encounters that test your skills
- **Secrets**: Hidden areas and collectibles for completionists
