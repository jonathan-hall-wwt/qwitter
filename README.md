# Best Books of 2020

A curated collection of the most acclaimed and influential books published in 2020.

## Fiction

### **The Vanishing Half** by Brit Bennett
A powerful novel about twin sisters who choose to live in very different worlds—one Black and one passing for white. A stunning exploration of identity, family, and the American experience.

### **Hamnet** by Maggie O'Farrell
Winner of the Women's Prize for Fiction, this historical novel imagines the life of Shakespeare's son Hamnet and the grief that inspired one of the greatest plays ever written.

### **The Mirror & the Light** by Hilary Mantel
The final installment of the Wolf Hall trilogy, completing the story of Thomas Cromwell. Winner of multiple awards and a masterpiece of historical fiction.

### **Shuggie Bain** by Douglas Stuart
Winner of the Booker Prize, this debut novel tells the heartbreaking story of a boy growing up in 1980s Glasgow with an alcoholic mother.

### **Transcendent Kingdom** by Yaa Gyasi
A profound exploration of science, faith, family, and addiction following a Ghanaian family in Alabama.

### **The Midnight Library** by Matt Haig
A thought-provoking novel about a library between life and death where each book represents a different life you could have lived.

## Non-Fiction

### **Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents** by Isabel Wilkerson
A groundbreaking examination of inequality in America through the lens of caste, comparing it to systems in India and Nazi Germany.

### **A Promised Land** by Barack Obama
The first volume of President Obama's memoirs, offering an intimate look at his presidency and the challenges of leadership.

### **The Splendid and the Vile** by Erik Larson
A gripping account of Winston Churchill's first year as Prime Minister during the London Blitz.

### **Minor Feelings** by Cathy Park Hong
A powerful essay collection exploring Asian American consciousness, race, and art in contemporary America.

### **The Code Breaker** by Walter Isaacson
The story of Jennifer Doudna and the revolutionary gene-editing technology CRISPR.

## Mystery & Thriller

### **The Guest List** by Lucy Foley
A locked-room mystery set at a wedding on a remote Irish island where secrets and lies lead to murder.

### **The Invisible Girl** by Lisa Jewell
A gripping psychological thriller about a missing girl and the dark secrets of a London neighborhood.

## Science Fiction & Fantasy

### **The City We Became** by N.K. Jemisin
A bold urban fantasy where New York City comes alive as human avatars must defend it against an otherworldly threat.

### **Piranesi** by Susanna Clarke
A mesmerizing fantasy about a man living in an infinite house of endless halls and ocean tides.

### **Network Effect** by Martha Wells
The first full-length novel featuring the beloved Murderbot, winner of the Hugo and Nebula Awards.

## Young Adult

### **Clap When You Land** by Elizabeth Acevedo
A novel in verse about two sisters who discover each other's existence after their father's death in a plane crash.

### **Cemetery Boys** by Aiden Thomas
A groundbreaking YA novel featuring a trans protagonist navigating family, identity, and unexpected romance.

## Poetry

### **The Hill We Climb** by Amanda Gorman
The powerful poem delivered at President Biden's inauguration, published as a collection celebrating hope and unity.

### **Postcolonial Love Poem** by Natalie Diaz
Winner of the Pulitzer Prize, a stunning collection exploring indigenous identity, desire, and survival.

---

*This list represents books that received critical acclaim, won major literary awards, or made significant cultural impact in 2020.*
