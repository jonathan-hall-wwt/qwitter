# Best Metroidvania Games

A curated list of the best Metroidvania games across all platforms. Metroidvania is a subgenre of action-adventure games that emphasizes exploration, non-linear progression, and the acquisition of new abilities that unlock previously inaccessible areas.

## What is a Metroidvania?

The term "Metroidvania" combines the names of two seminal franchises:
- **Metroid** (Nintendo) - Known for atmospheric exploration and ability-gated progression
- **Castlevania: Symphony of the Night** (Konami) - Popularized the formula with RPG elements

Key characteristics include:
- Interconnected world design
- Ability-gated progression (new powers unlock new areas)
- Backtracking and exploration
- Non-linear gameplay
- Character progression and upgrades

## The Classics

### Super Metroid (1994)
**Platform:** SNES, Switch (NSO)  
**Developer:** Nintendo

The gold standard that defined the genre. Features perfect atmosphere, tight controls, and masterful level design. Every area feels distinct, and the sense of isolation on planet Zebes is unmatched.

**Why play it:** The blueprint for the entire genre, still holds up today.

### Castlevania: Symphony of the Night (1997)
**Platform:** PlayStation, Xbox 360, PS4, Mobile  
**Developer:** Konami

Revolutionized the Castlevania series by adding RPG elements, equipment systems, and an inverted castle twist. Alucard's journey through Dracula's castle is iconic.

**Why play it:** Perfect blend of action, exploration, and RPG mechanics.

### Metroid Prime (2002)
**Platform:** GameCube, Wii, Switch  
**Developer:** Retro Studios

Successfully translated the Metroidvania formula into 3D first-person perspective. Incredible atmosphere and world-building.

**Why play it:** Proves the genre works brilliantly in 3D.

## Modern Masterpieces

### Hollow Knight (2017)
**Platform:** PC, Switch, PS4, Xbox One  
**Developer:** Team Cherry

A modern masterpiece with gorgeous hand-drawn art, challenging combat, and a hauntingly beautiful world. Hallownest is massive and filled with secrets.

**Why play it:** The best modern Metroidvania, with incredible value for money.

### Ori and the Blind Forest (2015) & Ori and the Will of the Wisps (2020)
**Platform:** PC, Xbox, Switch  
**Developer:** Moon Studios

Stunning visuals, emotional storytelling, and fluid movement mechanics. The sequel improves on the original in every way.

**Why play it:** The most beautiful Metroidvanias ever made, with touching narratives.

### Dead Cells (2018)
**Platform:** PC, Switch, PS4, Xbox One, Mobile  
**Developer:** Motion Twin

Blends Metroidvania exploration with roguelike elements. Fast-paced combat and procedurally generated levels keep each run fresh.

**Why play it:** Perfect for those who want more action and replayability.

### Metroid Dread (2021)
**Platform:** Switch  
**Developer:** MercurySteam

The first new 2D Metroid in 19 years. Intense E.M.M.I. chase sequences and smooth gameplay make this a worthy successor.

**Why play it:** Modern Metroid at its finest.

## Hidden Gems

### Haiku, the Robot (2022)
**Platform:** PC, Switch, PS4, PS5, Xbox

Charming pixel art and tight gameplay. A robot's journey through a mysterious world.

### Steamworld Dig 2 (2017)

### Yoku's Island Express (2018)
**Platform:** Multi-platform

Spiritual successor to Castlevania by Koji Igarashi. Deep crafting and shard systems.

## Tips for Newcomers

1. **Start with the classics** - Super Metroid or Symphony of the Night are perfect entry points
2. **Don't be afraid to explore** - Getting lost is part of the experience
3. **Take notes** - Mark areas you can't access yet for later backtracking
4. **Adjust difficulty** - Many modern games offer accessibility options
5. **Try different styles** - The genre has evolved to include many sub-genres

## Essential Features to Look For

- **Map system** - Good maps make exploration enjoyable
- **Fast travel** - Reduces tedious backtracking
- **Ability variety** - More abilities = more interesting progression
- **Boss quality** - Memorable bosses elevate the experience
- **Secrets** - Hidden areas and collectibles add replay value
